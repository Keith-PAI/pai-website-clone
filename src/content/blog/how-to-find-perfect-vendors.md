The vendors you choose for your conference can elevate the entire experience — or derail it. From AV providers to caterers to registration platforms, each vendor relationship matters.

## Start with Your Requirements

Before reaching out to vendors, document exactly what you need:

- **Scope of work** — What specific services do you require?
- **Timeline** — When do you need them, including setup and teardown?
- **Budget** — What can you realistically spend?
- **Standards** — What quality levels are non-negotiable?

## Where to Find Vendors

- **Venue recommendations** — Your venue likely has preferred vendor lists
- **Industry associations** — Organizations like MPI and PCMA maintain vendor directories
- **Peer referrals** — Other planners are your best source of honest reviews
- **Past experience** — Don't overlook vendors you've worked with successfully before

## Evaluation Criteria

When comparing vendors, consider:

- **Experience** with events similar to yours in size and type
- **References** from recent clients (and actually check them)
- **Insurance and certifications** appropriate for their services
- **Communication style** — responsiveness during the proposal phase predicts performance
- **Flexibility** — how they handle changes and unexpected situations

## Protect Yourself

Always use written contracts that specify deliverables, timelines, payment terms, and cancellation policies. Include performance standards and remedies if they aren't met. A good vendor relationship is built on clear expectations from the start.
