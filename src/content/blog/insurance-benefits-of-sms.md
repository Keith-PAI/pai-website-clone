For small 14 CFR part 135 charter operators, sightseeing operators under 14 CFR part 91.147, and part 21 manufacturers now required to implement SMS under part 5, an effective SMS can be the difference between an underwriter's hesitation and a lower premium.

## SMS as a Risk-Mitigation Strategy Insurers Can Quantify

Insurance underwriters assess incident and claim history, maintenance quality, pilot training, and organizational responsiveness. An organization with a documented, functioning SMS provides evidence of systematic risk management — exactly what underwriters look for.

## Alignment Builds Credibility

Alignment with Part 5 and ICAO Doc 9859 builds credibility with insurance providers, leading to better bids, broader coverage, and lower deductibles. When your SMS documentation demonstrates compliance with recognized standards, you're speaking the underwriter's language.

## Demonstrated Continuous Improvement

Meaningful Safety Performance Indicators (SPIs) show how the organization learns from data and reduces exposure over time. A demonstrated continuous improvement trend translates directly to a lower risk assessment at renewal time.

## SMS Data Creates Negotiating Leverage

At renewal time, organizations with robust SMS data can demonstrate:

- **Fewer incidents** leading to fewer claims
- **Proactive hazard identification** before issues become events
- **Documented risk controls** with evidence of effectiveness
- **Safety culture metrics** showing organizational commitment

## Manufacturers Benefit Too

Product-liability underwriters evaluate how design and production hazards are identified, mitigated, and tracked. A manufacturer's SMS demonstrates structured approaches to managing these risks throughout the product lifecycle.

## The ROI of Safety

The return on SMS investment extends across multiple dimensions:

- **Direct financial:** Fewer claims, lower premiums
- **Operational:** Fewer incidents, reduced disruption
- **Reputational:** Demonstrated safety commitment
- **Regulatory:** Compliance with expanding Part 5 requirements
- **Cultural:** Stronger safety culture throughout the organization

## Industry Voices

Leaders across the industry — including USAIG, ASQS GmbH, AIA, and NBAA — all cite SMS as a factor in better insurance outcomes. The consensus is clear: a well-implemented SMS is recognized by the insurance market.

## Getting Started

Draft a safety policy, identify hazards using risk matrices, document mitigations, track performance, and promote safety across your organization. Start small, but start now.

In the end, a robust SMS isn't just about compliance — it's about confidence.
