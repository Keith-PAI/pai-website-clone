Safety policy is the first component of SMS, and it's often treated as a formality — a document that gets signed, posted on a wall, and forgotten. But an effective safety policy does much more than convey management commitment. It serves as a practical decision-making tool.

## Beyond the Wall Poster

A strong safety policy provides clear guidance for decisions at every level of the organization. When employees face a choice between schedule pressure and safety, the policy should make the answer obvious. When managers allocate resources, the policy should inform priorities.

## Elements of an Effective Safety Policy

Under 14 CFR Part 5, a safety policy must include:

- **Management commitment** to safety as a core business function
- **Safety accountability** assigned throughout the organization
- **Key safety personnel** identified with defined responsibilities
- **Employee reporting policies** that protect reporters from retaliation
- **Clearly defined acceptable behavior** and unacceptable behavior

## Making It Real

The difference between a policy that works and one that doesn't often comes down to how it's communicated and reinforced. Leaders who reference the policy in daily decisions, who use it to explain resource allocation, and who hold themselves accountable to its standards create the conditions for authentic safety culture.

## The Connection to Culture

Safety culture isn't something you mandate — it emerges from consistent behavior aligned with stated values. Your safety policy defines those values. How well you live them determines your culture.
