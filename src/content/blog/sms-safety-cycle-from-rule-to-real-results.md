The four components of SMS don't operate in isolation — they form a continuous cycle that drives real safety improvements across your organization.

## The SMS Cycle

Each component feeds into the next, creating a self-reinforcing system:

1. **Safety Policy** establishes the commitment and framework
2. **Safety Risk Management** identifies and analyzes hazards
3. **Safety Assurance** verifies controls are working
4. **Safety Promotion** builds the culture that sustains it all

## From Rule to Results

The 2024 revision of 14 CFR Part 5 expanded SMS requirements, but compliance alone isn't the goal. Organizations that treat SMS as a living system — rather than a regulatory checkbox — see measurable improvements in safety outcomes.

The key is connecting your policy commitments to actual risk management activities, validating those activities through assurance processes, and communicating results through promotion efforts that strengthen your safety culture.

## Making It Practical

Start by mapping your current activities to each component. Where are the gaps? Where are the connections weak? A well-functioning SMS cycle means that lessons learned from safety assurance feed back into risk management, and safety promotion efforts are informed by real operational data.
