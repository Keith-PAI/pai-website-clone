A new year is the perfect time to commit to better meetings. Here are ten resolutions that will make a real difference.

## The Resolutions

**1. Every meeting gets an agenda.** No exceptions. If you can't write an agenda, you don't need a meeting.

**2. Start on time.** Respect the people who arrived promptly. Don't punish punctuality by waiting for latecomers.

**3. End on time — or early.** Ending five minutes early is a gift to everyone. It gives people time to prepare for their next commitment.

**4. Assign a facilitator.** Someone needs to keep the discussion on track. It doesn't always have to be the most senior person.

**5. Take real notes.** Action items, decisions made, and key discussion points should be documented and distributed within 24 hours.

**6. Invite only essential participants.** More people doesn't mean more productivity. Include decision-makers and key contributors; share notes with everyone else.

**7. Ban multitasking.** If the meeting isn't important enough for full attention, question whether it's important enough to hold.

**8. Make decisions.** Meetings should produce outcomes, not just discussions. End each topic with a clear decision or next step.

**9. Cancel unnecessary meetings.** If the agenda is empty or the key people can't attend, postpone or cancel. Protecting people's time builds trust.

**10. Ask for feedback.** Periodically ask participants what's working and what isn't. Continuous improvement applies to meetings too.

## Start Small

You don't have to implement all ten at once. Pick the three that would have the biggest impact on your organization and commit to those first. Small, consistent changes compound into dramatically better meetings.
