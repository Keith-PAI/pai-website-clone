Understanding your systems and operating environments is the foundational step of Safety Risk Management. Before you can identify hazards, assess risk, or develop controls, you need a clear picture of what you're managing.

## What Does "System Description" Mean?

A system description documents the components, interactions, and boundaries of a particular operation or process. It answers fundamental questions: What are we doing? How are we doing it? What people, equipment, and procedures are involved?

## Why This Step Matters

Organizations that skip or rush through system description often find their hazard identification incomplete. You can't identify what might go wrong if you don't fully understand how things are supposed to work.

## Operating Environment Considerations

Your operating environment includes:

- **Physical factors** — weather, terrain, airport characteristics
- **Organizational factors** — staffing, training, procedures, culture
- **Regulatory factors** — applicable rules, standards, and guidance
- **Technical factors** — equipment capabilities and limitations
- **External factors** — industry trends, economic pressures, supply chain

## Practical Application

Start with a manageable scope. You don't need to describe your entire organization at once. Focus on a specific operation, process, or system. Document the key components, how they interact, and the environment in which they operate. This becomes the foundation for everything that follows in SRM.
