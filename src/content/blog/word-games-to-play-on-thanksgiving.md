Holiday gatherings are the perfect opportunity to put down the screens and pick up some word games. Here are several that promote creativity, connection, and friendly competition — no special equipment required.

## Classic Word Games

**Boggle (or DIY Boggle)** — Shake up letter dice (or write random letters in a grid) and find as many words as possible in three minutes. Longer words score more points.

**Scrabble** — The classic tile-based word game. For larger groups, consider team play.

**Bananagrams** — Like Scrabble without the board. Each player builds their own crossword grid as fast as possible.

## No-Equipment-Needed Games

**Ghost** — Players take turns adding letters to a growing word fragment. The catch: you can't complete a word, and you must have a real word in mind.

**Categories** — Pick a category and a letter. Everyone has 60 seconds to list as many items in that category starting with that letter as they can.

**Story Chain** — One person starts a story with a sentence. Each person adds the next sentence. The results are often hilarious.

## For the Competitive Crowd

**Dictionary Game** — One player reads an obscure word from the dictionary. Everyone writes a fake definition. Mix in the real one and vote. Points for fooling others or guessing correctly.

Whatever you choose, word games are a wonderful way to bring people together over language and laughter.
